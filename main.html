Table of Contents

The Ozone Layer

  • situated in the stratosphery (10 km height)
  • UV rays crack oxygen, the radicals react to ozone
  • UV light cracks ozone, recombination with heat emission
    ⇒ UV absorption

  • coldness reduces ozone formation
  • some chemicals deplete the protecting ozone
    ⇒ ozone hole over the south pole
  • hole appears since 1985

The Ozone Hole

  • the ozone depletion remains stable
  • release of ozone depleting chemicals is reduced

Atmospheric Chemistry

  • radicals deplete ozone (halogens, nitric oxide)
  • CFC = chlorofluorocarbons
  • CFC need 15 years to move to stratosphery
  • CFC are cleaved by UV rays → free chlorine radicals
\[\mathrm{\mathbf{Cl}+O_3 \to ClO+O_2}\] \[\mathrm{ClO+O_3 \to \mathbf{Cl}+2\,O_2}\]
  • one chlorine radical can destroy 100 000 ozone molecules

Polar Nights

  • polar stratosphery clouds (PCS):
    crystals of H2SO4 and HNO3 arise in the winter months
  • PCS provide reaction surface:
    nitrogen compounds freeze and release chlorine compounds
  • more chlorine is activated
  • end of night: UV rays cleave free chlorine compounds

Ozone Depleting Chemicals

  • CFC was contained in sprays, refrigerants
  • halon: fire extinguishants
  • methyl bromide: antibiotic gas for quarantine or to make food sterile
  • great decrease due to political activity

Effects on Life

  • higher UV radiation exposure because of ozone hole
    → effects on plants, bacteria and animals
  • death of phytoplankton
  • high-energy rays are mutagens (cancer)
  • UV rays harm eyes and skin, sunburn is more likely

Political Interaction

Vienna Convention on the Law of Treaties (VCLT)
  • treaty according to international law
  • opened for signature on 22. May 1969
Montreal Protocol (appendant to the VCLT)
  • opened for signature on 16. September 1987
  • entered into force on 01. January 1989
Kyoto Protocol
  • specifies which states have to keep which amounts
  • adopted on 11. December 1997
  • entered into force on 16. February 2005
  • the only exception are the USA

Future Prospects

Different opinions
  • 2002 report from Japan → ozone hole will be closed 2040
  • other results indicate no improvement till 2065
  • diverse prospects for different ozone holes (e. g. Antarctic and Europe)
