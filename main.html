The Ozone Layer

  • situated in the stratosphery (10 km height)
  • UV rays crack oxygen, the radicals react to ozone
  • UV light cracks ozone, recombination with heat emission
    ⇒ UV absorption

  • coldness reduces ozone formation
  • some chemicals deplete the protecting ozone
    ⇒ ozone hole over the south pole
  • hole appears since 1985

The Ozone Hole

  • the ozone depletion remains stable

Atmospheric Chemistry

  • radicals deplete ozone (halogens, nitric oxide)
  • CFC = chlorofluorocarbons
  • CFC need 15 years to move to stratosphery
  • CFC are cleaved by UV rays → free chlorine radicals
\[\mathrm{\mathbf{Cl}+O_3 \to ClO+O_2}\] \[\mathrm{ClO+O_3 \to \mathbf{Cl}+2\,O_2}\]
  • one chlorine radical can destroy 100 000 ozone molecules

Polar nights

  • polar stratosphery clouds (PCS):
    crystals of H2SO4 and HNO3 arise in the winter months
  • PCS provide reaction surface:
    nitrogen compounds freeze and release chlorine compounds
  • more chlorine is activated
  • end of night: UV rays cleave free chlorine compounds
